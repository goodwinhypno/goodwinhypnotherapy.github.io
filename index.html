How Can Hypnosis Help Me?

"Live Like A Lion!"

Hypnosis is a state of focused attention and deep relaxation that can help manage symptoms and address psychological factors across a wide spectrum of conditions.

Mental Health Conditions

Anxiety disorders: Manage stress, panic attacks, and procedural anxiety by retraining brain responses.

Phobias: Reframe negative beliefs and use "imaginal exposure" to desensitize fears in a relaxed state.

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD): Safely process memories and improve emotional regulation.

Depression: Improve self-confidence and promote positive outlooks effectively.

Tinitus: Focus the mind on blocking irritating ringing noise from consciousness.

Insomnia and sleep disorders: Improve sleep quality and restorative slow-wave sleep cycles.

Chronic Pain & Procedures

Chronic pain: Conditions like fibromyalgia and back pain benefit from altered pain perception.

Procedural anxiety and pain: Reduce anesthesia needs and aid postsurgical healing for medical procedures.

Cancer treatment side effects: Alleviate side effects including pain, nausea, and vomiting.

Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS): "Gut-directed hypnotherapy" targets the brain-gut connection long-term.

Migraine and tension headaches: Successfully manage and alleviate pain from various headache types.

Behavior & Habit Changes

Smoking cessation: Address underlying habits and motivations in the subconscious mind.

Weight loss: Promote healthier eating habits and long-term weight loss maintenance.

Work/Executive Management Efficiency: Improve performance, satisfaction, and executive function.

Athletic Performance: Goal achievement and fighting burnout at professional and amateur levels.

Addictions: Reframe subconscious motivations by exploring underlying trauma.

Bed-wetting (enuresis): Proven therapeutic application for children.

Important considerations

Qualified Practitioner

It is crucial to work with a licensed healthcare professional, such as a psychologist or doctor, who has certified training in hypnotherapy.

Hypnotic Susceptibility

Not everyone is equally responsive to hypnosis. Some people are highly susceptible, while others may not enter a hypnotic state easily.

Complementary Therapy

For many conditions, hypnosis is most effective when used as part of a broader treatment plan that includes other interventions.
